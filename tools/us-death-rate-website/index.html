Overview

Current Status

The 2024 age-adjusted death rate of 722.0 per 100,000 is the lowest since 2020, indicating recovery from the pandemic's impact on mortality.

Key Trend

COVID-19 dropped from the top 10 leading causes of death in 2024, replaced by suicide as the 10th leading cause.

Projection Outlook

CBO projects mortality rates will continue declining through 2027, returning to long-term historical trends seen from 1950-2019.

Historical Death Rate Trends

The crude death rate per 1,000 population shows the impact of COVID-19 in 2020-2021, followed by recovery in subsequent years. The rate peaked at 10.42 in 2021 and has been declining since.
