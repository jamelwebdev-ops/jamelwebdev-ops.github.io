Executive Summary

Key findings for funeral industry professionals

This report provides funeral home owners with data-driven insights into US mortality trends and projections for strategic planning. Understanding these trends is essential for staffing, capacity planning, and business development.

Key Takeaway

After pandemic-era peaks, mortality rates are normalizing. The 2024 age-adjusted death rate of 722.0 per 100,000 is the lowest since 2020, signaling a return to pre-pandemic patterns with continued gradual decline through 2027.

With over 3 million deaths annually, funeral services remain in consistent demand despite rate fluctuations.

Strategic Recommendations

Action items for funeral home owners

Capacity & Staffing Planning

Maintain stable staffing levels — With ~3 million deaths projected annually through 2027, significant staffing changes are unnecessary. Plan for natural attrition.
Review facility capacity — Ensure your facilities can handle current volume with potential for 5-10% variability during seasonal peaks.
Cross-train staff — Flexibility in roles helps manage demand fluctuations without overstaffing.

Service Development

Expand grief counseling services — With suicide now in top 10 causes, families may need additional support services.
Develop specialized packages — Heart disease (22%) and cancer (20%) account for over 40% of deaths; consider tailored service offerings.
Alzheimer's/dementia services — Growing segment requiring sensitive family support and pre-planning services.

Business Development

Pre-need marketing focus — Aging population creates opportunity for pre-arranged funeral planning services.
Monitor acquisition opportunities — Market consolidation expected; smaller operators may seek exit strategies.
Healthcare partnerships — Build relationships with hospice providers, hospitals, and senior care facilities.

Data-Driven Decision Making

Use these projections as a foundation for your strategic planning. Monitor CDC quarterly updates for any significant changes in mortality patterns that may affect your local market.
